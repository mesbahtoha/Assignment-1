The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives
in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

"Bangladesh stands at the threshold of a promising future, driven by the dreams and determination of its people. Over the past decades, the country has transformed from a struggling economy into one of the fastest-growing in Asia. This momentum, if sustained with innovation and inclusivity, can lead Bangladesh toward becoming a developed nation by 2041. The vision for the future is built upon pillars like digital transformation, sustainable development, quality education, and equitable growth. With initiatives like "Digital Bangladesh" and advancements in sectors like garment manufacturing, agriculture, and renewable energy, the country is making impressive strides."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh

Recent News

On April 13, approximately 100,000 people gathered in Dhaka's Suhrawardy Park to protest Israeli actions in Gaza. The demonstration was part of a nationwide "Global Strike for Gaza," with participants calling for an end to the violence. Some multinational brand outlets were vandalized during the protests, accused of supporting Israeli policies.
